The Rapid Evolution of 4SP

Creation and Vision

4SP was founded on March 13, 2025. The initial vision was to provide a basic soundboard and sound request system for students.

The Turning Point (V4)

Version 4 (August 2025) represented a major shift, introducing the dashboard layout, widgets, Tab Disguising, and the Panic Key.

The Professional Rebuild (V5)

After a platform block in October 2025, Version 5 was created. It is a complete rebuild focusing on security and comprehensive student utility.

Project Brief

"This is the biggest, most complete summary of the 4simpleproblems project."

Overview

4simpleproblems, usually called 4SP, is a student-made web project built for bored school days. It mixes utilities, games, soundboards, and small tools that feel fast, local, and fun instead of polished or corporate. The project evolved from a couple of joke pages into a full dashboard-style platform with accounts, cloud functions, and serious security decisions.

Core Idea

  • Make something that feels personal and not "fake-professional."
  • Avoid corporate aesthetics.
  • Ensure it is fast to load and hard to block.
  • Provide entertainment when school is boring.

Tech Stack

  • Standard HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Firebase: Hosting, Firestore, Cloud Functions, and Auth.
  • Heavy use of CDNs (jsDelivr, Tailwind).
  • Minimal backend exposure to protect the client-side focus.

Version 4

Aug 22, 2025
The Dashboard Launch

Introduced the dark grey/purple scheme, a structured sidebar/dashboard, widgets, Tab Disguising, and the Panic Key. Proxies and other experimental pages were removed.

Version 3

The Visual Redesign

Shifted from a black-and-white to a light white-and-grey palette using the Outfit font, with minimal functional updates.

Version 2

Apr 11, 2025
The Great Expansion

Expanded significantly to 36 pages, introducing games, playlists, proxies, and multimedia. The repository became cluttered, and a pirated movie was later removed.

Version 1

Mar 13, 2025
The Genesis

Launched as a simple two-page website featuring a soundboard (22 normal, 4 explicit sounds) and a request page.

Final Thoughts

4SP is a long-running experiment and personal sandbox. It is a response to restrictions and a place where small tools matter. Every time someone tries to stop it, the project grows. It has history, users, and scars from blocks—and it is still changing.
